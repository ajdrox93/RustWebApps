Why it exists

Rust often turns a simple question into several calculator tabs, wiki searches, handwritten notes, or repeated in-game checking. Rust Web Apps was created to put those common planning jobs in one place.

The goal is not to tell anyone how to play. It is to remove the tedious math so players can spend more time building, farming, experimenting, and enjoying the game.

What you can plan

  • Building costs, upgrades, upkeep, doors, deployables, and storage
  • Electrical generation, power draw, industrial automation, farming, and water
  • General crafting, munitions, fireworks, quarry output, and cooking
  • Imported Fortify and CopyPaste builds for faster planner setup

Built for real use

The tools are designed around the kinds of questions that come up during an actual wipe: what to farm, what a build costs, whether a circuit has enough power, and how much material a group needs before crafting.

Features are added when they solve a useful problem rather than simply making the site larger.

Community feedback matters

Rust changes frequently, and even carefully checked data can eventually become outdated. Bug reports, corrections, and feature ideas help keep the tools accurate and useful.
